Shared purpose builds trust and strengthens loyalty.

Shared values and intentions can and will strengthen brand loyalty. Purpose is not a marketing exercise — it is the foundation of everything that follows. Customers want to believe in something. They want their wallets to be a vote, and their attention to be earned by something larger than the product itself.

not a company with a purpose.

Chapter 02
Tell me a story

Storytelling

Stories create connection, memory, and community.

A brand is a story that is always being told, shaped by narratives both real and imagined, memorable and impactful. While data can inform these stories, it does not define them. Founders and teams should tell your story — no one else will tell it the way you do.

The full brand storytelling map →
Strategy — who are we talking to and what do we need to shift?

Architecture — wound, protagonist, stakes, obstacle, turning point, voice, community, transformation.

Engineering — open loop, pattern interrupt, rule of 3, signpost, callback, metaphor, call to action.

Types — serialized, ephemeral, witnessed, live, immersive, embodied, agentic, transmedia.

Medium — platform, format, attention state, trust signal, AI layer.
"A brand is not what it says
about itself, but what a
friend tells a friend."

Chapter 05
Create defensibility

Network Effects
(NFX)

Network effects create defensibility and a durable moat.

Network effects are the #1 way to create defensibility and strong connection in the digital world. Scaled and engaged platforms have lower acquisition costs and build defensible moats around their communities. Each new participant makes the network more valuable to every other participant — that is the asymmetry.

Companies that leverage network effects have asymmetric upside. They punch above their weight. They are the Davids that beat the Goliaths — and then become the Goliaths.

— James Currier, NFX

What's your network effect? How are you building a movement?

Chapter 09
Unlocking joy

Receptional Design

Design moments of joy at the point of reception.

Receptional design is the art of transforming your customer's emotional state at the precise moment of engagement. It unlocks a moment of joy. It calms a moment of anxiety. It energizes a moment of learning. Emotions live in the brain — serotonin rises as anxiety drops, dopamine fires on achievement, endorphins energize, oxytocin builds trust. Receptional design deliberately activates these pathways.

Not designing for conception.
Designing for reception.
Omotenashi →
The Japanese concept of Omotenashi — to wholeheartedly look after your guest. Reception and exceptional, fused. The invisible micro-moments that make a guest feel anticipated, cared for, known.

Are you designing the invisible micro-moments, not just the experience itself? Does your product have automagical moments?

Factor 09
Receptional Design
The overlap of RECEPTION and EXCEPTIONAL. Brands can unlock joy. Joy is rooted in the brain. Experience design extends the customer journey through reception.
Chapter 10
Are you paying attention?

Engineering
for Virality

Build what spreads & amplify what's already moving.

Virality is behavior designed into the experience that people choose to share and propagate. It is not a great ad. It is not a big influencer. It is not paid distribution. It is the architecture of identity, emotion, and participation — engineered so that sharing feels like self-expression.

Virality = Identity × Emotion × Participation
The pattern underneath →
Participation ritual — belonging and FOMO simultaneously.

Personalization — identity expression disguised as sharing.

Conflict / sides — tribal loyalty and outrage both spread.

Brand character — entertainment value that earns attention.

Real-time relevance — first-mover cultural credibility.

Creative tool — the audience becomes the distribution engine.

What part of this makes someone look good sharing it? Where is the emotional spike? Can users participate, clip, or remix? What's the social payoff? Will they come back for more?

Factor 10
Engineering for Virality
Harness cultural trends, subcultures, and meme-driven dialogues. Foster relationship within the zeitgeist.
